Executive Summary

The Big 6 carriers are assumed to take a 15% rerate over the next 6–9 months. To stay in the ballpark, Wellabe targets 5% below their post-rerate average. Because we sit well below the Big 6 today, closing that gap requires sizeable increases — which reduce 2027 sales.

1 · Baseline — import & growth

Edit the baseline

All dollars in millions. The 2027 / 2026 % columns are the per-month growth ratio (e.g. 110 = 2027 is 110% of 2026); they default to 1 + growth above but are editable and drive 2027. To cut a state for a period, lower those months' % (e.g. IN Jan–Apr = 50). Edit a 2026 total (scales its months) or any 2026 monthly amount. State column is frozen; bottom row totals all states.

2 · Rate assumptions

Rate targeting

Target rate = Big 6 benchmark × (1 + rerate) × (1 + offset). Required increase is floored at 0% (we never assume cutting rates).

Sales projection

In policy-count mode the remaining policies each carry higher premium, so net premium = (1 − count reduction) × (1 + rate increase). The premium impact is smaller than the count drop — and where the rate increase outweighs the count loss, premium can even rise.

Elasticity — rate increase → policy-count reduction

A required increase of at least the threshold cuts that state's policy count by the reduction. Editable.
